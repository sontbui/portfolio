## The story arc

Son was born in **2003** in **Bao Loc, Lam Dong** — Vietnam's coffee highlands. His parents are coffee farmers, and he grew up inside the rhythm of that work: seasons you can't rush, harvests that reward patience, and problems you fix with your own hands because no one else will. He carried three things from that farm into engineering: **persistence** (coffee doesn't grow faster because you're frustrated), **discipline** (the work happens every day, not when inspired), and **humility** (nature doesn't care about your ego — and neither do production systems).

From Bao Loc he went to Ho Chi Minh City to study Computer Science at Open University (graduated **2025**). In **2024** — while still studying — he won **3rd prize in the faculty's scientific research competition** with a mobile food-ordering app whose YOLOv8-based food recognition could name a dish from a photo. He also worked professionally with a small team building **NLP models**, did a front-end contract at **Rovi Holdings**, and in **November 2024** joined **OPSWAT Vietnam** as an Associate SDET — the role that shaped his engineering identity.

## Personality, in practice

Son is open and easy to talk to — the kind of teammate you can raise a half-formed idea with. But in technical discussions he switches to a different gear: **direct, honest, truth over ego**. He'll tell you when he thinks a design is wrong, and he expects the same back. He is genuinely comfortable saying "I was wrong" — he treats mistakes as compressed lessons, not embarrassments. The sync bug in Beacon that wiped his own reminders became a design principle ("never let sync mean replace") and then a public case-study section. That's his pattern: mistake → principle → documentation.

He prefers **constructive debate over agreement**. A meeting where everyone nods worries him more than one with pushback.

## How he learns (and why it's fast)

Son's learning loop is **POC-first**: when something new appears, he doesn't study for weeks — he builds the smallest thing that could work, lets reality correct his misunderstandings, then iterates the design. Evidence across his work: he picked up the Claude Agent SDK, MCP, and n8n fast enough to ship an AI automation POC; learned Electron by shipping Beacon (278+ builds); learned YOLOv8 by shipping a prize-winning app. He'll tell you the first version is always partly wrong — the point is finding out *which part* quickly.

## How he works

- **Platforms over scripts:** if a problem will recur, he builds the system that solves the class of problems. His framework work at OPSWAT and both AI platforms follow this.
- **Honest engineering ledgers:** he names technical debt in writing (Beacon's README ships its own threat model). He believes documented weakness is a strength signal, not a weakness.
- **Human judgment stays human:** every AI system he builds has explicit human gates — approval before publishing test plans, review before merging PRs, opt-in before AI posts anything.
- **Developer experience matters:** he measures his platforms by whether the team actually adopts them, not by feature count.

## What drives him

The through-line of everything Son builds is **removing repetitive engineering work so humans can do judgment work**. The coffee-farm kid who watched his parents do unavoidably manual labor now builds systems so engineers don't have to. Near-term goal: **Senior SDET**. Long-term: designing **AI-powered engineering platforms** that combine AI, backend engineering, distributed systems, and testing — the intersection he's been circling from three directions (NLP models → test automation → AI products).

Outside work: **badminton** and **football**, played regularly and competitively enough to care about the score.

## Tóm tắt tiếng Việt

Sơn sinh năm **2003** ở **Bảo Lộc, Lâm Đồng** — thủ phủ cà phê. Ba mẹ làm nông, trồng cà phê; tuổi thơ đó cho Sơn ba thứ mang thẳng vào nghề kỹ sư: **kiên trì** (cà phê không chín nhanh hơn vì mình sốt ruột), **kỷ luật** (việc phải làm mỗi ngày, không phải khi có hứng), và **khiêm tốn** (hệ thống production không quan tâm cái tôi của mình). Học CNTT ĐH Mở TP.HCM (tốt nghiệp 2025), **Giải Ba NCKH 2024** với app nhận diện món ăn YOLOv8, từng làm NLP cho một nhóm nhỏ, contract front-end ở Rovi Holdings, và từ **11/2024 là SDET tại OPSWAT Việt Nam**.

Về tính cách: dễ gần, dễ trao đổi, nhưng khi bàn kỹ thuật thì **thẳng thắn, đặt sự thật trên cái tôi**, sẵn sàng nhận sai — với Sơn, sai lầm là bài học nén, và pattern quen thuộc của Sơn là: mắc lỗi → rút thành nguyên tắc → viết ra công khai. Cách học: **làm POC trước** — build bản nhỏ nhất chạy được để thực tế sửa hiểu lầm của mình, rồi mới tối ưu thiết kế; nhờ vậy học Claude Agent SDK, MCP, n8n, Electron, YOLOv8 đều rất nhanh. Động lực xuyên suốt: **loại bỏ việc lặp lại để con người làm việc cần phán đoán** — đứa trẻ lớn lên nhìn ba mẹ làm việc tay chân giờ xây hệ thống để kỹ sư đỡ phải làm việc tay chân. Mục tiêu gần: Senior SDET; xa hơn: thiết kế nền tảng kỹ thuật ứng dụng AI. Ngoài giờ: cầu lông và đá bóng.
