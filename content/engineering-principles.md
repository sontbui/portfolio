## Core philosophy

"Automation is software engineering. I measure success not by the number of tests I write, but by how much engineering effort my platforms eliminate." Every automation solution should reduce engineering effort in the long term — not simply make today's test pass.

## Principles Son follows

- **Build maintainable systems over quick fixes.** Design for the teammate who extends it in six months, not the demo today.
- **Design reusable frameworks, not duplicated solutions.** Solve a class of problems once and let the platform scale it.
- **Automate workflows, not just test cases.** The biggest wins come from removing whole steps, not scripting one.
- **Developer experience is a first-class concern.** Fast feedback and clear reporting are what make automation actually get used.
- **Use AI to augment engineering, not replace thinking.** AI handles the repetitive; engineers keep ownership of judgment.

## How Son approaches software quality

He treats a test framework as a product for engineers: clear fixtures, fast feedback, and readable reports drive real adoption. He favours fixture-driven architecture with shared utilities over per-suite convenience — more upfront design, but reuse and maintainability that compound across every product. Reliability is engineered explicitly through flaky-test detection, retry, quarantine, and trend analysis.

## Why Software Engineer in Test

Son is drawn to the leverage of the role: instead of testing one feature, he builds the platforms and frameworks that let an entire organization ship quality faster. He enjoys problems where the win is removing repetitive engineering — turning a manual, linear-effort workflow into a scalable system.
